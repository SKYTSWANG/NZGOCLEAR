1 | Project Introduction

New Zealand generates several million tonnes of woody waste each year from forestry, invasive species and municipal tree maintenance. This biomass causes erosion, flood damage and landfill methane yet is rarely valorised.

NZGOCLEAR solves the problem with mobile carboniser units that clear, carbonise and monetise waste on‑site, producing biochar and high‑integrity carbon‑removal credits while restoring land.

4 | Operational Plan

Pilot Region & Crew Model

Pilot region: Central Plateau (Taupō base) – proximity to Kaingaroa slash, wilding‑pine hotspots and state‑highway access.

Crew model: 4 FTE per unit: operator, loader driver, safety & client liaison; supported by 4‑t excavator or skid‑steer.

Throughput & Safety

Unit throughput: 1 t hr⁻¹ → 8 t per 10‑h shift; 260 shifts yr⁻¹ nominal.

Permitting: Fire & smoke consents under Clean Air regulations; wildfire plan; NZ$10 m liability insurance.

Measurement, Reporting & Verification (MRV)

Mass‑balance weighing, char carbon analysis and Puro.earth audit deliver traceable CORCs on a single registry interface across NZGOCLEAR.

5 | Market Analysis

  • Forestry slash: Gisborne alone produces ~500 000 t yr⁻¹; mandated removal averages NZ$145 t⁻¹.
  • Wilding conifers: 1.8 million ha infestations expanding 90 000 ha yr⁻¹; NZ$5.3 bn control cost over 20 years.
  • Gorse: ≈ 800 000 ha costing > NZ$20 m yr⁻¹ in lost productivity.
  • Urban tree waste: The three largest cities generate ≈ 350 000 t untreated wood annually.

6 | Distribution Method – Franchise‑Enabled Roll‑out

Our asset‑light franchise model unlocks rapid geographic expansion through local contractors, iwi trusts and forestry partners.

Why Franchise?

Local operators already have machinery, labour and customer relationships. A licence lets them layer biochar and CORCs onto existing services while NZGOCLEAR maintains consistent standards.

Target Franchisee Profiles
  • Arborist companies – year‑round biomass & council links.
  • Forestry contractors – slash handling mandated by regulation.
  • Civil & emergency firms – rapid mobilisation post‑storm.
  • Iwi trust enterprises – strong kaitiakitanga alignment.
